The Problem

We already live inside
a Panopticon.

The smartphone in your pocket, the platform you scroll, the city you walk through — each records more than you imagine. The debate about what to do about it has been stuck in a false choice.

🏙️

The Digital Panopticon Is Already Here

In the tradition of Foucault's analysis of Bentham's prison design, modern surveillance makes subjects permanently visible to centralized authority while that authority remains invisible to them.

  • Smartphones track location, contacts, and psychology 24/7
  • Facial recognition enables real-time individual tracking across cities
  • Social credit systems integrate all behavioral domains into a single score
  • Corporate platforms build psychological models more accurate than self-assessment
⚖️

The False Dichotomy: Freedom vs. Safety

Political debate treats more safety and more freedom as inversely correlated — a law of nature. It is not. The trade-off is real only when surveillance systems are designed to accumulate.

  • Retrospective targeting: archives become weapons for future regimes
  • Chilling effects: surveillance migrates into the imagination, suppressing dissent
  • Mission creep: tools built for safety consistently expand to control
  • The problem is not observation — the problem is accumulation

How It Works

Four steps. No archive.

Every EFA cycle completes in milliseconds. Raw sensor data never touches persistent storage. What happens in the edge device, stays in the edge device — then disappears.

👁️

Step 01

Sense

Camera or sensor captures the scene. Video frames load into volatile RAM only — no disk write, no persistent buffer.

🧠

Step 02

Analyze

Multiple independent AI models analyze the frame simultaneously. Each produces a confidence score for each detection category.

🔥

Step 03

Delete

Analysis complete. RAM buffer is overwritten with random data. Hardware Security Module issues a signed deletion attestation.

📡

Step 04

Signal

If models reach consensus, only an abstract signal is sent: event type + timestamp + location ID. No pixels. No faces. Nothing visual.

Key Principles

Five commitments
that make it different.

These are not policy promises. They are architectural constraints — encoded in firmware and open-source protocols that cannot be reversed by a board vote or an executive order.

🔇

Sense Without Store

Edge devices process video in real time but never persist recordings. The moment analysis completes, footage is cryptographically wiped. There is no archive to subpoena, hack, or misuse.

🤝

Multi-Model Consensus

No single AI model makes a detection decision alone. A minimum threshold of independent models — trained on different datasets and architectures — must agree before any signal is emitted.

⏱️

Ephemeral Deletion

Deletion is not optional or configurable by operators — it is hardcoded into device firmware and verifiable through open-source audit. The privacy guarantee is structural, not policy-dependent.

🗳️

Democratic Parameters

The list of "critical actions" that trigger a signal is defined through community deliberation, not vendor or government diktat. Parameters are versioned, auditable, and publicly debated.

📖

Open Source Architecture

All model weights, firmware, and consensus protocols are fully open source, enabling anyone to verify the system behaves as described and contains no hidden surveillance capabilities.

Why It Matters Now

The AGI era is approaching. The surveillance we build today will be available to it.

Artificial General Intelligence — AI systems capable of general reasoning and long-horizon planning — will make behavioral archives vastly more analytically powerful. An AGI with access to decades of accumulated surveillance data could identify hidden social networks, predict political behavior, and optimize persuasion at civilizational scale.

We have a window — perhaps a decade — to build structural commitments before that window closes. Architecture is more durable than policy. Firmware deletion is harder to reverse than a data retention guideline.

EFA Limits the Archive

Systems built on EFA principles generate minimal data for future AGI to exploit — by design, not by hope.

AGI as Compliance Auditor

Future AGI systems could audit EFA deletion logs and signal patterns to detect anomalies — turning the technology against its own potential misuse.

The Democratic Window is Closing

As AI capabilities increase, the political incentive to preserve privacy erodes. Structural choices made now will be the hardest to reverse later.
